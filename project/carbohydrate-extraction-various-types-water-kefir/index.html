Carbohydrate extraction from various types of water kefir
jan.jakub.nanista
Tue, 06/16/2015

Water kefir is a sour, alcoholic, and fruity fermented beverage of which the fermentation is started with water kefir grains. These water kefir grains consist of polysaccharide and contain the microorganisms responsible for the water kefir fermentation.

The aim of this work is to extract residual ethanol (or lactic acid, if milk kefir is used) from the resulting liquid to provide people with gastrointestinal problems such as SIBO a way to get required nutrients otherwise found in fruit. Alcohol content is responsible for the drink's sour taste, which many people can find unpleasant. More importantly, alcohol itself is digested and works as a substrate for small intestinal microflora to grow, producing effects similar to those of ingesting sugars, only with limited manifestation.
