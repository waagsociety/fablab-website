Lampfoot for TLB lamp
twschreuder
Wed, 02/25/2015

I made a lampfoot for the desklamp I designed using the lasercutter. I've drawn my file in illustrator. I measured all the parts that needed to be in the lampfoot (transformator, switch, weights magnets and bolts. The amount of layers cut is determined by the dept the items needed. I used Oaken plywood (available at Schmidt houthandel around the corner) and normal birch plywood. I laminated them all together and then and sanded them with a disk-sander and finally treated it with some dark furnishing oil. The result looks like a sold mass of oaken plywood. 

The lamp has several magnets placed underneath the toplayer. These magnets work through the wood and can stick paperclips to the surface. You can see in the picture that the paperclips are standing upright. The foot has a sunken switch which was made by measuring a existing switch and putting a wooden top on it. The rest of the lamp I completely made by hand using a no more than a drill and a beltsander. More info or pictures at http://www.thomschreuder.nl/tlblamp.html
