The Big Book of the Laser Cutter
womeijer
Fri, 11/20/2015

In order to cut things on the laser cutter in the best way, you need to cut at the right speed and power, to help zero that in, we keep a log of sucessful cuts for different materials as a starting point. Unfortunately, this only cosisted of two pieces of paper taped to a wall, which no one filled out and was infinitely messy.

In order to better organize the logs and encourage people to fill them in, I have created a book. Dubed "The Big Book of the Laser Cutter", it's based off of the Fabschoolino Box Project. The only changes were increasing the dimensions to fix A4 paper, changing the engraved decals, and adding holes for binder mounts.

The binder mounts are made according toÂ ISO 838, which is what the hole punches at our FabLab use. People in non-metric countries will need to change this for sure.
