Condoleances cards for blind friends
Debby
Tue, 07/14/2015

Normally I send a "Flower of Life", printed with "gold" ink on simili Japon paper, to friends and other people I care for if they have lost a beloved one. Last year, a few blind friends lost relatives, and I did mot have a personal card for them. I felt ashamed, so I decided to try to invent one.

Challenges:

- the burned fragrance of lasercut wood can be a stench in a funerary context. So, how do I solve this? I try different materials and ways to cut the figures.
- I am very visual, so, how do blind people experience my shapes? I will try that out in Autumn, when I meet visualy impaired collegues.
- Can the result be sent by snail mail, in an envelope?
- Does the name (and explenation if the ancient symbol) "Flower of Life" change the perception and aporeciation.

I attached a file with hand drawn Flowers of Life, a mathematical geometric shape. which I gave some "life" by drawing it by hand.
