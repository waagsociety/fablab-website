Lasercut lamp
dhooglandt
Sun, 06/28/2015

This was a project for school. I chose to use the lasercutter to achieve a clean and industrial look. The main inspiration source for the zip-tie construction is Alex' zip-tie bench which I helped making. I used 8 pairs of bright yellow LED's with a half-transparent piece of polycarbonate bolted on top. The inside is covered with aluminium foil to reflect more light. The lamp is adjustable in height for which I cut seven slots out, with a metal rod sliding in, thus acting as a lock for each position.

The wood used is 4 mm triplex for the body and 10 mm multiplex for the base. I mostly use hot glue to secure everything in place. Hope you like it!
