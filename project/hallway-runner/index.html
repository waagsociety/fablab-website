Hallway Runner
LucianoI
Tue, 10/13/2015

Inspired by an InstructableÂ project I came across, I decided to build an hallway runner light designed by FabricateIO.

Mine consists of an cold white LED strip, which is controled by an ATtiny chip mounted to a pcb I designed. There is also an PIR (motion) and an LDR (light) sensor connected to it. The PIR detects when somebody is walking through the hallway and de LDR detects the current lighting. The reason for the LDR is to make sure the led strip does not come on in the middle of the day when we move through the hall.

The PCB I made with Fritzing, afterwards I exported the gerber files and had it made by OSHPark.com.

After this all was done and worked, I now needed a casing to fit it all in. For the PIR sensor, I printed a case I found on the Thingiverse website.

The casing I am still trying to design. I'm trying to figure out how to get the right dimensions for the holes and spacers. For this, I will be using one of Fablabs 3D printers to test a couple of examples before I can continue. Afterwards I can also make an casing for the LDR sensor to complete my project.
