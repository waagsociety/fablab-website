Designing New Money at the Zondagsschilders
Debby
Thu, 04/23/2015

The Graphic Arts Group of the Zondagsschilders has examined the global cash flow problem and designed new banknotes. By printing fast forward we will soon generate sufficient cash to make the European balance positive.

I used the laser cutter in 2 ways:
- cutting through waxed cardboard (the facades)
- engraving text and the Amsterdam pylon on wood, making a stamp for printing

With the big milling machine I made a stamp for printing ('80' on the picture). I dyed the block with "80" on the big milling machine, as an experiment. To be honest, it was because the lasercutter is often occupied.

I did not know what to expect, and it was marvellous! The result is rugged, with a beard on the woodblocks. This inspired me to make spur gears for another project. In this little project the big milling was my biggest discovery.

As I am visualy impaired, I can not use electric equipment... unless it is a CNC machine! So, the FabLab offers me the opportunity I could not have dreamt of before I got there!
