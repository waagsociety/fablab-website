3D printing in the paramedical care
product | documentation
TwanvanDam
Fri, 03/13/2015

3D printing is a hot topic in the news at the moment and everyone seems to be interested when you’re talking about it. Seven paramedical care students (Felix van Balgooi, Twan van Dam, Ilona Valk, Daniëlle Klarenbeek, Eline van Schalkwijk, Lindy Steffens, Shahram Kosravi) of the minor Care Technology (at the Hogeschool van Amsterdam) had the opportunity to work with it. We were asked to investigate the possibilities of 3D printing in the paramedical care.

To see if paramedics could use the 3D printer themselves, and to see if they can create objects themselves, we decided to make our own finger splint ourselves. We did some research which software would be the best for us. We tried 2 different modelling software programs and evaluated them. Blender and Rhinoceros were evaluated, but we weren't satisfied after that. We thought it was too hard for paramedics to use and it took too much time to learn, so Grasshopper (an add-on for Rhinoceros) was used to make the 3D model easily adjustable.
