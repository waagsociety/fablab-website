Drawing experiments
Debby
Tue, 07/14/2015

As I worked with the milling machine, it inspired me to draw the model differently, with the possibilties I discovered so far in mind. This poses both limitations and new ways.

I want to make a kinetic statue, and spur gears are perfect for that purpose. In the gears I would like to put figurines without weakening the gear.

First I experimented with models within a rest shape. I tryed to connect the solid body to the plane with lines. I made the lines to thin, so the drill "ate" them and the shape fell out (partly). Than I designed a "Hollow" body with lines to the plane, to make sure the gears would remain strong enough to endure stress and torque, etc.

So, the body kind of causes a light within a dark plane. I wanted to find out how many details could survive within the drawing in a milled work. The lines inside the light body must be bold enough to survive a 3 mm drill...

Next I milled comparable figurines within an outline, so, not within a plane. After that I experimented with solid figurines without a plane around them. It is really fun to experiment with shapes. I could not have thought of this before I went to the Fablab. It is a present.

My Fablab moments shows the process.
